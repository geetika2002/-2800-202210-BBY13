What is sustainable material and why is it important?

Sustainable materials are ones that can be produced in various volumes without the use of non-renewable resources (e.g. plastic); they essentially grow naturally. These materials prevent unneccessary wastage and pollution in our environment during mass constructions, and/or small time renovations.

What are some examples of sustainable materials?

Remember that sustainable materials are usually grown naturally, but many materials are not. A couple examples of these include: bamboo, pre-cast slabs, recycled plastic, reclaimed/recycled steel, and plant based polyurethane rigid foam. All these materials are either made from renewable sources or are recycled, making them eco-friendly and waste reducing.

Is the usage of sustainable materials popular?

Want to hear some good news? According to the World Green Building Trends 2018 report from Dodge Data and Analytics, green building is officially a “global trend” and energy conservation is a top priority for people all over the planet. In the U.S., the shift is being driven by consumer demands, followed by environmental regulations, and healthier buildings.
